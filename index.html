it was stolen glances at first. nothing more than intrigue, a whisper of attraction. in between coffee sips, pilfered looks under innocent pretenses. we'd both caught the other staring, but there was nothing to be thought of it. he must lock eyes with every other pretty girl that walks by. but then, against my better judgement, it became routine. a part of my day. a much anticipated part, even. as soon as i rounded the corner, i would look for you. and somehow, your eyes would find mine, no matter the crowd. these looks i purloined were like small pieces i was puzzling together to solve the riddle that was you. the familiarly unfamiliar bend in your neck when you laughed at something you found particularly funny. the perpetual look on your face that suggested you were trying to solve something unsolvable. the confident air of mystery you had around you. your arrogance, and the way you looked at me. you would turn to look at me with eyes that already knew i was watching you. for weeks, i raged an internal debate with myself trying to decipher the expression on your face. was it lust? curiousity? indifference? some days i would go so far as to speculate it was jealousy, at seeing me with another boy. i thought about you, a complete stranger, more than i'd like to admit. and then one day, after a long day of not seeing you, before i knew what i was doing, before the neurons in my brain could signal the muscles in my face to stop, i saw you and i smiled at you. i smiled at you? and for some reason, i couldn't stop. i couldn't stop smiling at the bewildered expression on your face, all the while internally kicking myself. you must've thought i was touched in the head.

you disappeared for a bit after that, to my dismay. i asked around about you, any attempt at subtelty being sabotaged by my friends' antics, as you very well know. i began losing hope. but then, that one story, and that one reply.

that was the beginning of the first year of the rest of our lives.
